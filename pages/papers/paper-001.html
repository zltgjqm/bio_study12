Spatial transcriptomics identifies CXCL13-driven macrophage niches in fibrotic lung

Kim J, Alvarez R, Chen L, et al. · Nature · 2025

TL;DR

Spatial profiling of fibrotic lung tissue shows CXCL13-high macrophages clustering into discrete niches near tertiary lymphoid structures, linking a chemokine signal to local immune organization. Worth reading in full if you work on macrophage heterogeneity or TLS formation.

Spatial Lung Macrophage

Main findings

CXCL13-high macrophages were spatially enriched in fibrotic regions and consistently co-localized with markers of tertiary lymphoid structures, suggesting these macrophages actively participate in organizing local immune aggregates rather than being passive bystanders.

Methods

Spatial transcriptomics applied to fibrotic lung tissue sections, paired with single-cell reference mapping to assign cell identities to spatial spots.

Claims & evidence

Claim: CXCL13 expression marks a distinct macrophage niche. Evidence: spatial co-clustering of CXCL13 transcript density with macrophage markers, replicated across multiple tissue sections in the dataset.

Limitations

Single-tissue-type cohort; causal role of CXCL13-high macrophages in TLS formation (versus recruitment toward pre-existing TLS) is not established by this spatial snapshot alone.
